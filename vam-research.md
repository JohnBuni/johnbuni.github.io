# Volumetric Additive Manufacturing Research

## Overview
This project summarizes my undergraduate research in **Volumetric Additive Manufacturing (VAM)**, a novel 3D printing process capable of fabricating entire parts simultaneously through tomographic light projection.

## Objective
The goal of this research was to understand and optimize how resin properties and process parameters influence curing behavior, dimensional accuracy, and achievable part size in a tomographic VAM system.

## Experimental Approach
Experiments were conducted using a projector-based volumetric additive manufacturing setup with SLA-type photopolymer resins. The process involves projecting a sequence of 2D imagesâ€”derived from slicing a 3D CAD modelâ€”into a resin volume so that the object cures simultaneously in three dimensions.

### Key Variables Investigated
- Photo-initiator concentration (wt%)
- Resin mixing time prior to exposure
- Thermal conditions during resin preparation
- Exposure parameters and part geometry

### Performance Metrics
- Dimensional accuracy  
- Cure completeness  
- Curing time  
- Observed failure modes  

## Key Findings
The experiments and analytical modeling established clear relationships between resin penetration depth and achievable part dimensions in a volumetric additive manufacturing process. Experimental measurements supported the model, demonstrating how material properties and system geometry constrain curing behavior and feasible object size.
